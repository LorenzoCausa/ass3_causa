 Finds the two possible poses of a square planar object given its four corner correspondences in an image using IPPE. These poses are sorted so that the first one is the one with the lowest reprojection error. The second pose is needed if the problem is ambiguous. The problem is ambiguous when the projection of the model is close to affine, which in practice happens if it is small or viewed from a large distance. In these cases there are two pose solutions that can correctly align the correspondences (up to noise), so it is impossible to select the right one from just the reprojection error. IPPE gives you both the solutions, rather than just a single solution (which in ambiguous cases would be wrong 50% of the time). Geometrically, the two poses roughly correspond to a reflection of the model about a plane whose normal passes through the line-of-sight from the camera center to the model's center.
